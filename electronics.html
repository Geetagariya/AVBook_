Electronics Engineering (Diploma)

Electronics Engineering diploma focuses on circuit design, embedded systems, microcontrollers, and electronics devices. Students learn to design and maintain electronic systems for industries.

Core Topics

  • Electronic Devices & Circuits
  • Microprocessors & Microcontrollers
  • Digital Electronics
  • Embedded Systems
  • Communication Systems

Career Opportunities

Graduates can work as Electronics Engineers, Embedded System Designers, Maintenance Engineers or pursue higher studies in Electronics and Communication Engineering.

About Lab

Analog electronics lab

This lab focuses on fundamental components like diodes, transistors, and operational amplifiers. Students use basic test and measurement tools like multimeters, oscilloscopes, and function generators to build and test simple circuits.

Digital Electronics and Microprocessor Lab

Students learn about digital logic circuits, including logic gates, flip-flops, counters, and registers. These labs often feature dedicated "trainer kits" with pre-wired setups for performing experiments and programming microprocessors.

Communication Lab

This is where students experiment with the principles of analog and digital communication systems. They test different modulation techniques using a range of equipment.
